           Rumor, Reputation and Legend System

           Version 0.1

           1. DESIGN    PHILOSOPHY

           The player does not control their reputation.

           The world creates its own version of the player's story.

           Truth and rumors are different things.

           Every action creates:

              • witnesses;
              • stories;
              • rumors;
              • legends;
              • myths.

           2. INFORMATION       PROPAGATION

           Information spreads through:

              • travelers;
              • merchants;
              • soldiers;
              • bards;
              • criminals;

              • priests;
              • letters;
              • rumors.
           The speed of information depends on:

              • distance;
              • importance;

              • political interest.

           3. INFORMATION       ACCURACY

           Every story has an accuracy level.

           Examples:

           100%

              • exact truth.

           75%

              • mostly true.

           50%

              • heavily distorted.
           25%

              • mostly false.
           0%

              • complete fabrication.

           4. RUMOR     CREATION

           Example:

           Real event:

           The player defeats three bandits.

           Possible rumors:

           Version 1: "He defeated five bandits."

           Version 2: "He slaughtered twenty men."

           5. WITNESSES

           Witnesses determine rumors.

           Witness types:

              • honest;
              • fearful;
              • drunk;
              • fanatical;
              • hostile;
              • friendly.

           The same event produces different stories.

           6. BARDS

           Bards amplify stories.

           Example:

           Reality:

           Player killed a wolf.

           Ballad:

           "The fearless warrior fought the beast for hours."

           16. TITLES

           Examples:

           "The Wolf Killer"

           "The One-Eyed Warrior"

           Examples:

21. NPC   MEMORY

           NPCs remember:

              • conversations;
              • promises;
              • crimes;
              • heroism;
              • humiliation.

           days, months, years.

           22. FAMILY    MEMORY

           Example:

           23. HISTORICAL     MEMORY

           Major events enter history.

           Examples:

           Wars. Massacres. Heroes. Disasters.

           Books may mention them.

           Possible stages:

           25. AI NPC   USAGE

           NPCs use:

              • rumors;
              • memories;
              • legends;
              • personal opinions.

           Example:

           Guard:

           "I heard you defeated the beast."

           Merchant:

           "I heard you cheated."

           Priest:

           "I heard you are cursed."

           All may refer to the same event.

           26. CORE   RULE

           The player does not create their own legend.

           The world creates the player's legend.

           And the world may be wrong.
