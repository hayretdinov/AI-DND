# AI-DND Injury, Poison and Disease System

AI-DND

           ADDENDUM   —  INJURY, POISON AND  DISEASE SYSTEM

           Version 0.1

           1. DESIGN    PHILOSOPHY

           Combat consequences must persist.

           A battle is not:

              • win;
              • lose;
              • heal instantly.

           A battle creates consequences.

           Examples:

              • scars;
              • broken bones;
              • infections;
              • poison;

              • trauma;
              • disabilities.

           2. BODY   PART   SYSTEM

           Every character has independent body zones.

              • Head
              • Torso
              • Left Arm
              • Right Arm
              • Left Leg
              • Right Leg

           Each body part has:

              • health;

              • bleeding;

           3. HEAD   INJURIES

           Possible effects:

           Minor:

              • dizziness.

           Moderate:

              • concentration penalties.
           Severe:

              • hallucinations;
              • memory loss;
              • unconsciousness.
           Critical:

              • death.

           4. ARM   INJURIES

           Possible effects:

           Minor:

              • reduced accuracy.

           Moderate:

              • slower attacks.

           Severe:

              • unable to use weapon.

              • crawling only.

              • reduced stamina.

7. BLEEDING

           Bleeding severity:

              • light;
              • medium;
              • severe;
              • fatal.

           Effects:

              • HP loss;
              • weakness;
              • unconsciousness;
              • death.

           Treatment:

              • bandages;
              • medicine;
              • healers;
              • magic.

           8. FRACTURES

           Possible fractures:

              • arm;
              • leg;
              • ribs;
              • skull.

           Effects:

              • combat penalties;
              • movement penalties;
              • permanent consequences.

           Healing:

              • days;
              • weeks;
              • months.

9. PAIN  SYSTEM

           Pain levels:

           No Pain ↓ Discomfort ↓ Pain ↓ Severe Pain ↓ Agony

           Effects:

              • accuracy penalties;
              • slower actions;

              • fear;
              • panic;
              • unconsciousness.

           10. POISON    SYSTEM

           Poison types:

              • weak poison;
              • hunting poison;
              • assassin poison;
              • paralytic poison;
              • magical poison.

           Possible effects:

              • health loss;
              • paralysis;
              • blindness;
              • hallucinations;
              • weakness;
              • death.

           11. DISEASE    SYSTEM

           Diseases exist permanently in the world.

           Examples:

              • fever;
              • plague;
              • lung infection;

              • blood parasites;
              • magical corruption.

           12. INFECTION     SYSTEM

           Untreated wounds may become infected.

           Symptoms:

              • fever;
              • weakness;
              • pain;
              • delirium.

           Treatment:

              • medicine;
              • herbs;
              • healers;
              • magic.

           13. MENTAL     CONDITIONS

           Characters may suffer:

              • fear;
              • panic;

              • trauma;
              • nightmares;
              • madness.
           Examples:

           After dragon encounter:

              • fear of dragons.
           After torture:

              • PTSD.

After magical corruption:

              • hallucinations.

           14. SCARS

           Major injuries create scars.

           Scars affect:

              • appearance;
              • reputation;
              • dialogue.

           Example:

           NPC:

           "You survived a troll attack? I recognize those scars."

           15. PERMANENT       INJURIES

           Some injuries never fully heal.

           Examples:

              • lost eye;
              • broken arm;
              • damaged leg;
              • crippled hand.

           Players may learn to adapt.

           16. PLAYER    INFORMATION

           The player never sees:

           "Left Leg HP = 32"

           Instead:

           17. AI DESCRIPTION

           AI describes injuries naturally.

           Example:

           Instead of:

           -20 HP.

           AI says:

           "The sword cuts deep into your thigh. Your leg trembles, and every step becomes painful."

           18. CORE   RULE

           Every injury must tell a story.

           Combat should create memories, not just reduce numbers.
