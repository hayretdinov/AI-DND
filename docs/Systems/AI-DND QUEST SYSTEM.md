AI-DND

           QUEST_SYSTEM.md

           Dynamic Quest System

           Version 0.1

           1. DESIGN    PHILOSOPHY

           Quests are not missions.

           Quests are problems.

           The world creates problems.

           The player decides whether to become involved.

           2. QUEST   ORIGIN

           Quests emerge from:

              • NPC goals;
              • NPC fears;
              • world events;
              • economics;
              • diseases;
              • wars;
              • rumors;
              • player actions.

           4. NO  QUEST    GIVERS

           NPCs do not have:

           "!"

           above their heads.

           Players discover quests through:

              • conversations;
              • observation;
              • rumors;
              • events.

           5. QUEST   DISCOVERY

           Examples:

           Player hears:

           "My daughter disappeared."

           Possible quest created.

           Player sees:

           Village burning.

           Possible quest created.

           6. QUEST   STRUCTURE

           Every quest contains:

              • problem;
              • participants;
              • goals;
              • risks;
              • consequences;
              • expiration.

           7. QUEST   EXPIRATION

           Quests may expire.

           Examples:

           Missing child:

           3 days.

           Plague:

           2 weeks.

           War:

           months.

           8. QUEST   FAILURE

           Failure creates consequences.

           Examples:

           9. QUEST   SUCCESS

           Success creates consequences.

           Examples:

           Save village:

              • gratitude;
              • prosperity;
              • reputation.

           10. HIDDEN     QUESTS

           Some quests remain hidden.

           Examples:

              • conspiracies;
              • cults;
              • secrets.

           11. MULTIPLE     SOLUTIONS

           Every quest may have multiple solutions.

           12. MORAL     CONSEQUENCES

           No universal morality exists.

           Actions affect:

              • opinions;
              • factions;
              • reputation.

           13. PLAYER    CREATED    QUESTS

           Players may create goals.

           Examples:

              • revenge;
              • exploration;
              • investigation.

           14. NPC   GENERATED      QUESTS

           NPCs create quests naturally.

           Example:

           Farmer loses harvest.

           Problem created.

23. QUEST    MEMORY

           NPCs remember:

              • success;
              • failure;
              • promises;
              • betrayal.

           24. QUEST    JOURNAL

           The journal records:

              • observations;
              • memories;
              • clues.

           Not objectives.

           25. JOURNAL     EXAMPLE

           Instead of:

           "Kill 5 wolves."

           Player sees:

           "The hunter believes wolves are attacking travelers near the old forest road."

           26. PLAYER    ABSENCE

           Quests continue during Stone Sleep.

           Examples:

              • wars continue;
              • people die;
              • cities fall.

           Rumors may create quests.

           28. AI-DM   ROLE

              • consequences;
              • complications.

           29. QUEST    IMPORTANCE

           Minor ↓ Important ↓ Major ↓ Historical

           30. CORE   QUESTION

           The player never asks:

           "What quest should I do?"

           The player asks:

           "What should I do?"

           31. CORE   RULE

           Quests are not content.

           Quests are the result of a living world.
