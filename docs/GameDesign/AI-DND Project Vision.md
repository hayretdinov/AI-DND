# AI-DND Project Vision

AI-DND

           PROJECT VISION DOCUMENT

           Version 0.1

           Status: Approved Foundation

           1. PROJECT    OVERVIEW

           Working Title

           AI-DND

           Genre

           Single-player RPG with AI-driven NPCs.

           The game combines:

              • dark fantasy atmosphere;
              • tabletop D20 mechanics;

              • dynamic AI characters;
              • world exploration;
              • text-based interaction;
              • tactical combat.

           Core Inspiration

           The project takes inspiration from:

              • Gothic
              • Dungeons & Dragons
              • Battle Brothers
              • Darkest Dungeon
              • Kingdom Come: Deliverance

           The goal is NOT to copy these games, but to reproduce their strongest emotional experiences.

2. GAME   PHILOSOPHY

           The player is NOT:

              • the chosen one;
              • a hero;
              • a savior;
              • a legendary warrior.

           The player begins as an ordinary person.

           The world does not revolve around the player.

           Respect, power, influence and knowledge must be earned.

           3. MAIN   FEATURE    OF  THE   GAME

           The primary feature of the game is NOT graphics.

           The primary feature is:

           Living AI Characters

           Every NPC:

              • has memory;
              • has personality;
              • has goals;
              • has fears;
              • has relationships;
              • remembers conversations;
              • reacts to player actions;
              • exists independently from the player.

           4. PLAYER   INTERACTION       SYSTEM

           There are NO dialogue buttons.

           The player always enters free text.

           Example:

                                          2

<!-- Page 3 -->

"I slowly draw my sword."

               "I try to convince the guard."

               "I offer the merchant a better deal."

               "I attack his left arm."

           The player describes intentions.

           The game engine determines:

              • what the player is trying to do;
              • whether the player can do it;
              • the probability of success;
              • the result.

           The AI then narrates the outcome.

           5. GOLDEN     RULE   OF  THE  PROJECT

           The player describes intentions.

           The game engine calculates results.

           The AI describes consequences.

           Formula:

           Player Intent ↓ Intent Parser ↓ Rule Validator ↓ Game Engine ↓ Dice Roll System ↓ AI Narrator ↓ Player
           Response

           6. WORLD    STRUCTURE

           The game world consists of:

              • one continent;
              • multiple regions;
              • settlements;
              • roads;
              • wilderness;
              • ruins;

• dungeons;
              • hidden locations.

           The world exists independently from the player.

           7. GAMEPLAY     LOOP

           Travel ↓ Random Event ↓ Dialogue / Exploration / Combat ↓ Reward / Consequence ↓ Character
           Development ↓ Travel

           8. COMBAT     PHILOSOPHY

           Combat is:

              • dangerous;
              • tactical;
              • slow;
              • meaningful.

           Combat is NOT:

              • arcade;
              • action-based;
              • fast-paced.

           The player fights through decisions.

           9. D20  SYSTEM

           Every important action uses dice.

           Examples:

              • persuasion;
              • intimidation;
              • attacks;
              • defense;

              • stealth;
              • lockpicking;
              • magic;
              • crafting;

                                          4

<!-- Page 5 -->

• investigation.

           The player may not always see the dice rolls.

           10. BODY    PART  TARGETING

           Characters can attack body parts.

           Possible targets:

              • head;
              • torso;
              • left arm;
              • right arm;

              • left leg;
              • right leg.
           However, body targeting depends on training.

           Example:

           Novice:

              • torso only.

           Student:

              • torso;
              • arms.

           Experienced:

              • torso;
              • arms;
              • legs.

           Master:

              • all body parts.

           11. TRAINING     SYSTEM

           Characters do not gain abilities automatically.

                                          5

<!-- Page 6 -->

Abilities are learned from teachers.

           Examples:

              • one-handed swords;
              • two-handed swords;
              • axes;
              • maces;
              • daggers;
              • bows;
              • crossbows;
              • magic schools.

           Without training, advanced techniques are impossible.

           12. WEAPON      TYPES

           Every weapon has damage categories.

           Examples:

              • slashing;
              • piercing;
              • crushing;
              • fire;
              • frost;
              • poison;
              • holy;
              • shadow.

           Armor protects differently against each type.

           13. WORLD     LORE   PRINCIPLES

           The world must feel:

              • ancient;
              • dangerous;
              • mysterious;
              • believable.
           There are no purely good factions.

                                          6

<!-- Page 7 -->

There are no purely evil factions.

           Everyone believes they are right.

           14. PLAYER    KNOWLEDGE

           The player does not receive information through UI markers.

           Information is acquired through:

              • rumors;
              • observation;
              • dialogue;
              • exploration;
              • books;
              • experience.

           Example:

           The player discovers teachers by speaking to people.

           15. AI NPC   RULES

           AI NPCs:

              • cannot break lore;
              • cannot invent impossible facts;
              • cannot override game rules;
              • cannot change dice results;
              • cannot create items from nothing;
              • cannot know hidden information.

           16. GAME    OVER   PHILOSOPHY

           Failure is part of the story.

           There is no save-scumming design philosophy.

           The world remembers mistakes.

           Visual style:

              • dark fantasy;
              • Gothic atmosphere;

              • medieval realism;
              • old parchment aesthetics;
              • wood and iron interfaces;
              • warm fire lighting;
              • cold wilderness lighting.

              • progression.

           Phase 3:

              • AI NPCs;

           Phase 4:

              • world simulation;
              • economy;
              • factions;
              • events.

19. PROJECT    MISSION

           Create a role-playing game where:

              • the player can attempt anything;
              • the world reacts logically;
              • NPCs behave like living people;
              • every playthrough creates unique stories.

           This project aims to build a living fantasy world rather than a traditional RPG.
