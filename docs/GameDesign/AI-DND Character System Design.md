           1. CHARACTER      PHILOSOPHY

           The player is not a hero.

           The player is not chosen.

           The player begins as an ordinary person.

           Power must be earned.

           Knowledge must be learned.

           Respect must be deserved.

           2. CHARACTER      CREATION

           At the beginning of the game the player creates:

              • name;
              • gender;
              • appearance;
              • origin;

              • personality tendencies.
           The player does NOT choose:

              • class;
              • profession;

              • destiny.
           These emerge naturally during gameplay.

3. CHARACTER      ORIGINS

           Possible origins:

              • Prisoner
              • Mercenary
              • Hunter
              • Blacksmith Apprentice
              • Farmer
              • Refugee
              • Soldier
              • Thief
              • Scholar
              • Orphan

           Origin determines:

              • starting reputation;
              • knowledge;
              • equipment;
              • memories;
              • contacts.

           5. HIDDEN    ATTRIBUTES

           Characters also possess hidden traits.

           Examples:

              • bravery;
              • greed;
              • compassion;
              • cruelty;

              • curiosity;
              • discipline;
              • ambition.
           These influence AI reactions.

           6. EXPERIENCE

           Characters gain:

           Learning Points (LP)

           LP are earned through:

              • quests;
              • discoveries;
              • survival;
              • combat;
              • exploration;
              • training.
           LP are spent with teachers.

           8. SKILL  LEVELS

           Untrained ↓ Novice ↓ Student ↓ Experienced ↓ Master ↓ Legend

           Players see names, not numbers.

           9. TRAINING

           Skills cannot be learned automatically.

           Requirements:

              • teacher;
              • LP;
              • money;
              • reputation.

           10. REPUTATION

           Three systems exist.

           Personal Reputation

           How individual NPCs see the player.

Local Reputation

           Faction Reputation

           How organizations react.

           11. FEAR

           Characters develop fears.

           Examples:

              • fear of spiders;
              • fear of darkness;
              • fear of magic;
              • fear of dragons;
              • fear of death.

           12. TRAUMA

           Traumatic experiences create:

              • nightmares;
              • hesitation;
              • panic;
              • depression;
              • aggression.

           13. MORALITY

           There is no morality meter.

           Instead:

           NPCs judge actions.

Example:

           Murder:

              • some respect you;
              • some fear you.

           14. HEALTH

           Health consists of:

              • body condition;
              • mental condition;
              • injuries;
              • diseases;
              • fatigue.

           15. BODY    SYSTEM

           Body zones:

              • head;
              • torso;
              • left arm;
              • right arm;
              • left leg;
              • right leg.

           Each body part:

              • may be injured;
              • may heal;
              • may become disabled.

           16. SCARS

           Scars remain permanently.

                                          8

Examples:

              • burned face;
              • missing eye;
              • damaged hand;
              • broken nose.

           17. ADDICTIONS

           Characters may develop addictions.

           Examples:

              • alcohol;
              • drugs;
              • magical substances.
           Effects:

              • benefits;
              • penalties;
              • social consequences.

           18. FATIGUE

           Fatigue levels:

           Rested ↓ Tired ↓ Exhausted ↓ Collapsed

           Fatigue affects:

              • combat;
              • dialogue;
              • travel;
              • learning.

           19. HUNGER

           Hunger levels:

Satisfied ↓ Hungry ↓ Starving

           Effects:

              • weakness;
              • disease risk;
              • poor decisions.

           20. AGE

           Characters age.

           Age affects:

              • attributes;
              • reputation;
              • health.

           21. MEMORY

           The player character also has memory.

           The game remembers:

              • promises;
              • enemies;
              • friends;
              • crimes;
              • achievements;
              • failures.

           22. KNOWLEDGE

           The character only knows what they learned.

           Examples:

           Unknown language: cannot read.

           Unknown creature: cannot identify.

Unknown disease: cannot diagnose.

           23. SOCIAL    STATUS

           Possible status:

              • nobody;
              • known traveler;

              • respected citizen;
              • noble;
              • criminal;
              • legend.

           24. LEGENDARY      STATUS

           Legendary status is earned through:

              • deeds;
              • stories;
              • reputation.

           Not through levels.

           25. CORE   RULE

           The player does not become stronger because of experience.

           The player becomes stronger because they learn, survive, adapt, and change.
