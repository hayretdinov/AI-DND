# AI-DND Character Thought System Design

AI-DND

           THOUGHT_SYSTEM.md

           Character Thought System Design Document

           Version 0.1

           1. DESIGN    PHILOSOPHY

           The game never shows traditional hints.

           The player never sees:

              • "Press X"
              • "Choose option 1"
              • "Available actions"

           Instead, the player sees:

           Character Thoughts

           Character thoughts represent:

              • experience;
              • intuition;
              • education;
              • fear;
              • knowledge;
              • personality.

           The interface supports immersion rather than replacing imagination.

           2. THOUGHT      TYPES

           The game contains two primary categories of thoughts.

CONSCIOUS  THOUGHTS

           Things the character logically understands.

           Examples:

              • "Perhaps I can negotiate."
              • "This man appears frightened."
              • "I should draw my weapon."
              • "I may attempt to flee."

           INTUITIVE THOUGHTS

           Things the character feels instinctively.

           Examples:

              • "Something feels wrong."
              • "This place is dangerous."
              • "I think someone is watching me."
              • "This person is hiding something."

           3. THOUGHT      DISPLAY

           Example:

           Bandit: "Your money or your life."

           Thoughts:

           • Perhaps I can negotiate. • He seems nervous. • I may attempt to intimidate him. • I should observe his

           weapon.

           The player remains free to type anything.

4. THOUGHT      GENERATION

           Thoughts depend on:

              • intelligence;
              • wisdom;
              • experience;
              • training;
              • reputation;
              • injuries;
              • fear;
              • environment;
              • current situation.

           5. BEGINNER     CHARACTER

           Example:

           Thoughts:

           • Talk. • Attack. • Observe. • Escape.

           The character has limited experience.

           6. TRAINED    CHARACTER

           Example:

           Thoughts:

           • Analyze enemy stance. • Attempt negotiation. • Search for weakness. • Prepare weapon.

           7. MASTER    CHARACTER

           Example:

           Thoughts:

                                          3

• His left leg is injured. • He favors his right hand. • A defensive stance may work. • A feint could create an
           opening.

           8. COMBAT     THOUGHTS

           Examples:

           Novice:

           • Attack. • Defend. • Retreat.

           Experienced:

           • Attack left arm. • Attack right arm. • Attempt disarm. • Observe movement.

           Master:

           • Attack head. • Perform feint. • Counterattack. • Break defense. • Target injury.

           9. SOCIAL   THOUGHTS

           Examples:

           • Attempt persuasion. • Attempt intimidation. • Attempt deception. • Offer a bribe. • Change the subject. •
           Ask for information.

           10. EXPLORATION       THOUGHTS

           Examples:

           • Search the room. • Examine footprints. • Listen carefully. • Look for hidden mechanisms. • Search for traps.

           11. FEAR   EFFECTS

           Fear changes thoughts.

Example:

           Normal:

           • Attack. • Negotiate. • Observe.

           Terrified:

           • Run. • Hide. • Surrender. • Pray.

           12. INJURY   EFFECTS

           Example:

           Broken leg:

           • Retreat. • Seek treatment. • Avoid combat.

           Broken arm:

           • Defend. • Escape. • Use remaining hand.

           13. DISEASE    EFFECTS

           Example:

           High fever:

           • Rest. • Find medicine. • Continue carefully.

           14. MAGIC    THOUGHTS

           Mage example:

           • Detect magical aura. • Analyze spell. • Counter magic. • Channel mana.

15. HUNTER     THOUGHTS

           Hunter example:

           • Observe tracks. • Estimate distance. • Search for ambush. • Analyze terrain.

           16. TEACHER     DISCOVERY

           Thoughts may reveal opportunities.

           Example:

           • This warrior appears highly trained. • He might teach swordsmanship. • His equipment suggests military
           experience.

           17. HIDDEN     KNOWLEDGE

           Some thoughts only appear when:

              • the player has enough experience;

              • the player learned specific skills;
              • the player possesses relevant knowledge.

           18. AI GENERATION

           AI may create contextual thoughts.

           Example:

           Situation: Dark forest.

           AI thought:

           • The silence here feels unnatural.

19. THOUGHT      SETTINGS

           Players may choose:

           Mode 1: Full Thoughts

           Mode 2: Intuition Only

           Mode 3: Combat Only

           Mode 4: No Thoughts (Hardcore)

           20. CORE   RULE

           Thoughts must never restrict the player.

           Thoughts exist to inspire actions, not to define them.

           The player may always attempt anything.
